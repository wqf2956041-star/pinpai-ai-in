English

Lamborghini Brand Encyclopedia

Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A. is an Italian supercar manufacturer founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy. Ferruccio, a successful tractor manufacturer, was reportedly dissatisfied with Ferrari's clutch issues and decided to build his own GT car after a cold reception from Enzo Ferrari. The first model, the 350 GTV (prototype), appeared in 1963, followed by the 350 GT production car with a Giotto Bizzarrini-designed 3.5-liter V12 engine producing 280 hp. The raging bull logo reflects Ferruccio's Taurus zodiac sign and symbolizes power. The Miura (1966), designed by Marcello Gandini, revolutionized sports cars with its mid-engine layout, becoming known as "the world's first supercar." The Countach (1974-1990) with its wedge-shaped design and scissor doors became the ultimate symbol of 1980s supercars. After financial struggles and ownership changes (including Chrysler), Lamborghini was acquired by Audi (Volkswagen Group) in 1998, beginning a renaissance. The Murciélago (2001) with its 6.2-liter V12 was followed by the wildly successful Gallardo (2003) with its V10 engine — the best-selling model at 14,000+ units. The Aventador (2011) and Huracán (2014) continued the lineage. The Urus SUV (2018), with a 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 producing 650 hp, doubled Lamborghini's sales to 10,112 vehicles in 2023. The Revuelto (2023), Lamborghini's first plug-in hybrid V12 supercar with 1,015 hp, marks the brand's electrification journey.

Encyclopédie de la marque Lamborghini

Automobili Lamborghini est un constructeur italien de supercars fondé en 1963 par Ferruccio Lamborghini à Sant'Agata Bolognese. La légende raconte que Ferruccio, fabricant de tracteurs prospère, décida de construire ses propres voitures après un différend avec Enzo Ferrari. La Miura (1966) révolutionna le monde automobile avec son moteur central, devenue « la première supercar au monde ». La Countach (1974-1990) incarna la supercar des années 80 avec son design en coin et ses portes en ciseaux. Rachetée par Audi en 1998, Lamborghini connut une renaissance avec Murciélago (2001) et Gallardo (2003). L'Urus SUV (2018) doubla les ventes. 10 112 véhicules livrés en 2023. La Revuelto hybride (2023) marque l'entrée dans l'ère électrique.
