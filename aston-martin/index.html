Aston Martin Brand Encyclopedia

Aston Martin Lagonda Global Holdings PLC is a British luxury GT and supercar manufacturer founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford in London. The brand name combines Martin's surname with his hill climb victory at Aston Clinton in Buckinghamshire. The first Aston Martin car, the Coal Scuttle (1915), featured a 1.4-liter Coventry Simplex four-cylinder engine. After seven bankruptcies in its history, Aston Martin was acquired by industrialist Sir David Brown in 1947, ushering in the modern era. The DB2 (1950), DB4 (1958), and DB5 (1963) defined the brand — the latter became the most famous car in cinema as James Bond's vehicle in Goldfinger (1964), featuring gadgets like machine guns, ejector seat, and smoke screen. The DB7 (1994) became one of the brand's best-selling models. The DB9 (2004) introduced the VH aluminum platform and V12 engine. The Vanquish (2012) served as the flagship super-GT. The DBX SUV (2016) marked the brand's expansion beyond sports cars. The Valkyrie (2020), co-developed with Red Bull Advanced Technologies, features a 6.5-liter Cosworth V12 hybrid producing 1,176 hp. The DB12 (2023) is marketed as "the world's first super GT" with a 671 hp Mercedes-AMG V8. Aston Martin entered Formula 1 as a works team in 2023, with Fernando Alonso achieving multiple podiums. The brand is synonymous with British craftsmanship, understated luxury, and James Bond's enduring legacy.
