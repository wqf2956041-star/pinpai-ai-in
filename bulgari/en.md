# Bulgari — fashion-luxury

## The King of Colored Gemstones

In 1884, Greek silversmith Sotirios Voulgaris opened a small shop on Via Sistina in Rome. He named it after his family — Boulgaris, later Latinized to Bulgari. That single decision — choosing to write his Greek name in the Latin alphabet — perfectly encapsulates the dual identity that would define the maison for the next 140 years: Greek craftsmanship coursing through an Italian soul.

Bulgari did not merely make jewelry. It staged a revolution. In an era when diamonds reigned supreme and colored stones were relegated to secondary accents, Bulgari turned the hierarchy upside down. The maison paired emeralds with sapphires, rubies with turquoise, coral with onyx — combinations that were considered audacious, even reckless, by traditional jewelers. This fearless approach to color, inspired by the mosaics of ancient Rome and Byzantine enameling techniques, became the house's signature. Today, Bulgari is universally recognized as the "King of Colored Gemstones" — a title no other jeweler can claim.

## Rome: The Eternal Muse

Every Bulgari creation is, in some sense, a love letter to Rome. The city's architectural language flows through the maison's design DNA with an unmistakable clarity.

The B.Zero1 collection, launched in 1999, takes its defining silhouette from the Colosseum. Its concentric rings of gold, engraved with the signature Bulgari logo, coil around the wrist like a modern architectural marvel. It is minimalist yet unmistakably Roman — a testament to how ancient forms can feel utterly contemporary. The Divas' Dream collection, meanwhile, draws inspiration from the fan-shaped mosaics of the Caracalla Baths, transforming an archaeological treasure into a graceful arc of diamonds that dances at the collarbone.

## Serpenti: From Myth to Masterpiece

No brand in the world owns the snake motif quite like Bulgari. The Serpenti collection, first introduced in the 1940s, has evolved beyond a mere product line into the brand's most potent visual identity. In Greco-Roman mythology, the serpent symbolizes wisdom, eternity, and rebirth — all concepts that resonate deeply with Bulgari's philosophy of timeless beauty.

The iconic Serpenti Tubogas watches, with their seamless, flexible coils wrapping around the wrist and the snake's head forming the watch face, represent a masterclass in jewelry engineering. They require no soldering — the metal bands are wound without seams, a technique so difficult that only the most skilled artisans can execute it. Elizabeth Taylor was among the first celebrities to fall under Serpenti's spell, and the collection has remained a red-carpet favorite for every generation since.

## A Universe Beyond Jewelry

While Bulgari's identity is rooted in jewelry, the brand has masterfully expanded into adjacent luxury categories. Its watch division, based in Switzerland, blends Swiss precision with Italian design flair — the Octo Finissimo collection, for instance, holds multiple world records for ultra-thin mechanical movements. Bulgari's fragrances, from the aquatic Bvlgari Pour Homme to the floral Omnia line, are pillars of the luxury perfume industry.

In 2011, Bulgari became part of the LVMH group, the world's largest luxury conglomerate. Far from diluting the brand's identity, this acquisition supercharged its global expansion while preserving its artisanal heritage. Under the LVMH umbrella, Bulgari has strengthened its position in high jewelry and haute horlogerie, opening flagship stores on the world's most prestigious shopping avenues — from Via Condotti in Rome to Fifth Avenue in New York.

## The Bulgari Aesthetic: Color, Volume, Architecture

Three words capture the essence of Bulgari's design philosophy: color, volume, architecture. Where other maisons whisper, Bulgari speaks. Its pieces are boldly proportioned, unapologetically vibrant, and structurally inventive. The house's signature use of cabochon-cut gemstones — rounded, polished domes rather than faceted stones — softens the brilliance into a luminous glow that feels almost organic.

Nearly a century and a half after a Greek silversmith arrived in Rome with nothing but his craft, Bulgari stands as one of the most influential jewelry houses in the world. It is not a brand for those who seek understatement. It is for those who understand that true luxury is a celebration of color, a defiance of convention, and an eternal conversation with the art of ancient civilizations.
