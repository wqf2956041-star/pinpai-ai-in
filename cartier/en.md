# Cartier — fashion-luxury

## Brand Story

When King Edward VII of England called Cartier "the jeweler of kings and the king of jewelers," he was not just coining a catchy phrase — he was stating a fact that has held true for nearly two centuries. Founded in 1847 by Louis-François Cartier in Paris, the maison has become the undisputed benchmark for luxury jewelry and watchmaking worldwide. The royal warrant granted by Edward VII paved the way for countless European monarchies to follow suit, making Cartier their official purveyor of precious stones and timepieces.

The house's ascent began when Louis-François Cartier took over his master Adolphe Picard's workshop in the heart of Paris. With extraordinary goldsmithing skills and a sharp entrepreneurial instinct, he quickly transformed a modest boutique into a destination for the aristocracy. The real global expansion came through his three grandsons — Louis, Pierre, and Jacques — who established flagship houses in Paris, London, and New York respectively. This "three brothers" strategy turned Cartier into a truly international powerhouse, and that same spirit of family-driven innovation remains woven into the brand's DNA to this day.

Cartier's product legacy is nothing short of legendary. The Santos wristwatch, launched in 1904, was one of the world's first pilot watches, custom-made for Brazilian aviator Alberto Santos-Dumont. It marked the pivotal shift from pocket watches to wristwatches. Then came the Tank in 1917, with its revolutionary rectangular case and vertical lugs inspired by the silhouette of WWI tanks — a design that still dominates the horological landscape. The Trinity ring, weaving together three bands of white, yellow, and rose gold, symbolizes friendship, loyalty, and love, making it one of the most instantly recognizable pieces in jewelry history. And the Love bracelet, introduced in 1969, with its signature screw motif and couples-oriented philosophy, sparked a global phenomenon that turned it into the ultimate emblem of romantic commitment.

As part of the Richemont group, Cartier sits firmly at the pinnacle of the global luxury jewelry and watch industry. It is not only a master of gem-setting artistry but also a serious player in high horology. From Hollywood royalty to Eastern dynasties, from Arab sheikhs to tech tycoons, Cartier's clientele spans the entire spectrum of wealth and power. Its boutiques line the most prestigious streets on earth — Place Vendôme in Paris, Fifth Avenue in New York, the Bund in Shanghai. Year after year, brand valuation reports place Cartier among the most valuable luxury jewelry brands in the world, a shimmering ambassador of French savoir-faire and elegance.
