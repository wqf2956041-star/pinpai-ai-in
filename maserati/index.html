English

Maserati Brand Encyclopedia

Maserati S.p.A. is an Italian luxury automobile manufacturer founded in 1914 by Alfieri Maserati in Bologna, Italy. One of the oldest racing car manufacturers, Maserati is known for its trident logo (from the Neptune statue in Bologna), Italian design, and distinctive engine sound. The first Maserati race car was the Tipo 26 (1926), which won its class at Targa Florio. In 1939, an 8CLT won the Indianapolis 500 — the only Italian manufacturer to do so. Juan Manuel Fangio won the 1957 F1 World Championship driving a Maserati 250F. The company shifted to GT road cars in the late 1950s. Key models include the Mistral (1963), Ghibli (1967), and Bora (1971). Acquired by Fiat Group in 1993, Maserati modernized with the 3200 GT (1998), Quattroporte (2003), and GranTurismo (2005). The Levante SUV (2013) became the brand's best-seller. The MC20 supercar (2020) with the in-house Nettuno V6 engine featuring F1-derived pre-chamber combustion technology marks Maserati's return to the supercar segment. The GranTurismo Folgore (2022) is Maserati's first all-electric model with a tri-motor system. Maserati sold approximately 26,000 vehicles globally in 2023, positioning itself as a smaller but exclusive player in the ultra-luxury segment.
