劳斯莱斯品牌百科

劳斯莱斯（Rolls-Royce）是世界上最负盛名的超豪华汽车制造商，1906年由查尔斯·罗尔斯和亨利·罗伊斯在英国曼彻斯特创立。品牌以"追求完美"为信条，百年来始终站在汽车工艺和奢华的巅峰。1904年罗尔斯和罗伊斯在曼彻斯特米德兰酒店首次会面达成合作协议。1906年公司正式成立并推出银魂（Silver Ghost）车型，搭载7.0升直列六缸发动机，以惊人的平顺性和静音性被誉为"世界上最好的汽车"。1925年幻影系列诞生。1931年劳斯莱斯收购宾利。1949年银云推出。1965年银影推出。1998年宝马集团收购劳斯莱斯品牌使用权，在古德伍德建立了全新工厂。2003年全新幻影VII推出，采用铝合金空间框架结构和6.75升V12发动机。此后产品线不断扩展——古斯特（2008）、魅影（2013）、曜影（2015）、库里南（2018）。2023年闪灵（Spectre）作为品牌首款纯电动车型推出。2023年劳斯莱斯全球销量创下6032辆的历史新高。

Rolls-Royce Brand Encyclopedia

Rolls-Royce Motor Cars is a British ultra-luxury automobile manufacturer established in 1906 by Charles Rolls and Henry Royce in Manchester, England. The brand motto "Strive for Perfection" has guided the company for over a century as it consistently produced vehicles considered the finest in the world. In 1904, Rolls and Royce met at the Midland Hotel in Manchester and agreed to build cars together — Royce designed and manufactured, Rolls sold and promoted. The company formally incorporated in 1906 and launched the legendary Silver Ghost (40/50 hp) with a 7.0-liter inline-six engine, famed for its remarkable smoothness and silence, remaining in production until 1926. Rolls-Royce rapidly gained an international reputation for engineering excellence, producing armored cars and aircraft engines for the British and Allied forces during WWI. The Phantom series debuted in 1925, establishing Rolls-Royce's dominance in the ultra-luxury segment. In 1931, Rolls-Royce acquired Bentley Motors, sharing chassis and engine technology between the brands until 2002. Iconic design elements include the Parthenon-style grille, the Spirit of Ecstasy hood ornament (created by sculptor Charles Sykes in 1911, modeled on Eleanor Thornton), and the double-R badge. The Silver Cloud (1949) was the first Rolls-Royce with a factory-built standard steel body. Throughout the 1950s and 1960s, Rolls-Royce Phantom V and Phantom VI were adopted by royalty and heads of state worldwide, including Queen Elizabeth II. The Silver Shadow (1965) introduced monocoque construction and independent rear suspension, becoming the highest-volume Rolls-Royce model ever at over 30,000 units. A pivotal moment came in 1998 when BMW Group acquired the rights to the Rolls-Royce brand name and logo for £40 million, while Volkswagen Group bought Bentley and the Crewe factory. BMW built an entirely new factory in Goodwood, West Sussex, launching the all-new Phantom VII in 2003, featuring an aluminum spaceframe, a 6.75-liter V12 engine, and unparalleled interior luxury. The product line expanded with the Ghost sedan (2008), the Wraith coupe (2013), the Dawn convertible (2015), and the Cullinan SUV (2018). The Spectre, Rolls-Royce's first all-electric vehicle launched in 2023, features a 120 kWh battery pack, dual-motor all-wheel drive producing 577 hp, and an estimated range of 520 km. The Bespoke customization program is central to the brand, offering over 44,000 paint colors and limitless interior combinations. Each vehicle is hand-assembled by craftspeople at Goodwood. In 2023, Rolls-Royce achieved its highest-ever annual sales of 6,032 vehicles, with Cullinan leading sales and Spectre orders exceeding expectations. The brand remains the definitive symbol of automotive luxury and exclusivity, with Phantom prices starting above $450,000 before Bespoke customization.
