Bentley Brand Encyclopedia

Bentley Motors Limited is a British ultra-luxury automobile manufacturer founded in 1919 by Walter Owen Bentley (W.O. Bentley) in London. The brand's motto "Be Extraordinary" reflects its commitment to combining grand touring luxury with exceptional performance and motorsport heritage. W.O. Bentley was a railway engineer and racing enthusiast who had designed BR.1 and BR.2 aircraft engines during WWI. In 1919 he founded Bentley to build "the world's fastest lorry" — fast, durable luxury cars. The first production Bentley, the 3-Litre (1921), featured an advanced 3.0-liter four-cylinder engine with four valves per cylinder, immediately setting records at Brooklands. The 1920s were Bentley's golden age of motorsport — the Bentley Boys (wealthy British gentleman racers including Tim Birkin and Woolf Barnato) won the 24 Hours of Le Mans an unprecedented five times (1924, 1927, 1928, 1929, 1930), most famously with Barnato's Speed Six in 1930. During the Great Depression, Bentley was acquired by Rolls-Royce in 1931. The two brands shared chassis and V8 engine platforms until 2002. The 1952 R-Type Continental is considered the first true four-seat grand tourer, renowned for its 100+ mph cruising capability. In 1998, Volkswagen Group acquired Bentley and the Crewe factory for £430 million, while BMW got Rolls-Royce. VW invested heavily in Crewe. The 2003 Continental GT coupe, with a 6.0-liter twin-turbo W12 engine producing 560 hp, transformed Bentley's fortunes — from near-extinction to the leader in the ultra-luxury segment. The product line expanded with the Continental Flying Spur sedan (2005), Continental GTC convertible (2006), the flagship Mulsanne (2009), the second-generation Continental GT (2012), and the Bentayga SUV (2015) — the latter quickly became the world's best-selling ultra-luxury SUV. The "Beyond100" strategy announced in 2020 plans full electrification by 2030. Bentley delivered 13,560 vehicles globally in 2023, with Flying Spur, Continental GT, and Bentayga each accounting for roughly one-third of sales. The Mulliner division offers extensive customization, continuing the legacy of the world's oldest coachbuilder.
